The Trinity of Volatility, Operations & Labor

VOL Health™ is a directional diagnostic, not a survey. It scores staffing “health” across ten volatility drivers and ties them back to the three forces every operator feels daily: volatility, operations, and labor.

Pillar 1

Volatility (census, acuity, PBJ, survey risk)

Where are census swings, acuity spikes, and documentation gaps creating unpredictable pressure on the building? VOL Health surfaces where variability is quietly turning into regulatory and financial risk.

Pillar 2

Operations (leadership bandwidth, reputation, liability)

How much leadership time is being hijacked by staffing fires? The model treats ED and DON bandwidth, incidents, and community reputation as operating assets — not afterthoughts.

Pillar 3

Labor (OT, call-outs, partner model)

Overtime, call-outs, and partner sprawl are translated into a single stability view so operators can see when traditional staffing approaches have become a volatility engine instead of a safety net.

Three Stability Views at a Glance

Every run of VOL Health™ plots a building or portfolio into one of three directional bands. The goal isn’t a “perfect score” — it’s a clear, shared language for where volatility is acceptable, emerging, or actively extracting value.

Dashboard Skin

Low Stability Risk

Stability scores in the 70–100 range. Volatility is present but managed. Leadership can focus on growth, culture, and referrals instead of constant staffing triage. VOL Health highlights where to protect this position as census, acuity, and PBJ expectations shift.

Dashboard Skin

Mixed Stability

Stability scores in the 40–69 range. Operations are working, but OT, PBJ confidence, and reputation are absorbing real strain. This is the “early action” band — targeted elasticity on weekends and pain shifts can materially move the score.

Dashboard Skin

High Volatility

Stability scores below 40. Volatility, survey exposure, and leadership drag are likely extracting hundreds of thousands of dollars in hidden cost. VOL Health reframes the conversation from “rate” to risk, safety, and avoided downside.

How VOL Health™ Works

The calculator converts ten operational signals — census stability, acuity swings, overtime, PBJ accuracy, survey exposure, reputation, leadership bandwidth, and partner model — into a stability score and directional economics. It’s intentionally lightweight: 5–7 minutes per run for a single building, or a portfolio roll-up in one pass.

The framework is a prototype built for executive conversations — not a regulatory tool, not a staffing mandate. It’s designed to make volatility visible, quantify the upside of elasticity, and give EDs, DONs, and VPs Ops a shared language for the hidden costs they already feel every week.
