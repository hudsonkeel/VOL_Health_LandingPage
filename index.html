The Trinity of Volatility, Operations & Labor

VOL Health™ is a directional diagnostic, not a survey. It scores staffing “health” across ten volatility drivers and ties them back to the three forces every operator feels daily: volatility, operations, and labor.

Pillar 1
Volatility (census, acuity, PBJ, survey risk)
Where are census swings, acuity spikes, and documentation gaps creating unpredictable pressure on the building? VOL Health surfaces where variability is quietly turning into regulatory and financial risk.
Pillar 2
Operations (leadership bandwidth, reputation, liability)
How much leadership time is being hijacked by staffing fires? The model treats ED and DON bandwidth, incidents, and community reputation as operating assets – not afterthoughts.
Pillar 3
Labor (OT, call-outs, partner model)
Overtime, call-outs, and partner sprawl are translated into a single stability view so operators can see when traditional staffing approaches have become a volatility engine instead of a safety net.

Three Stability Views at a Glance

Every run of VOL Health™ plots a building or portfolio into one of three directional bands. The goal isn’t a “perfect score” – it’s a clear, shared language for where volatility is acceptable, emerging, or actively extracting value.

Dashboard skin
Low Stability Risk
● Low Volatility Zone
Stability scores in the 70–100 range. Volatility is present but managed: OT is contained, PBJ is clean, leadership is not constantly pulled into staffing fires. Focus shifts to protecting this position as census and expectations evolve.
Dashboard skin
Mixed Stability
● Watchlist Zone
Stability scores in the 40–69 range. Operations work, but OT, call-outs, or PBJ anxiety are quietly taxing the building. A verified elasticity layer on weekends and pain shifts can pull this cohort back toward the green.
Dashboard skin
High Volatility
● Red Flag Zone
Stability scores below 40. Volatility, survey exposure, and leadership burnout are likely extracting real dollars every month. Here, the question isn’t “Should we do something?” – it’s “What’s the fastest, safest way to reduce downside risk?”

How VOL Health™ Works with StaffHealth + Accushield Verified

VOL Health™ doesn’t replace your staffing strategy – it quantifies where an elasticity layer would do the most good, then links that stability gain to an illustrative economic range.

Step 1
Score today’s volatility
In ten questions, VOL Health™ captures census, acuity, OT, PBJ confidence, survey risk, incidents, reputation, leadership bandwidth, partner model, and reimbursement pressure for a single community or a full portfolio.
Step 2
Model elasticity impact
You choose how aggressively to deploy StaffHealth as an elasticity layer – conservative, moderate, or strategic. The model converts that choice into stability points gained and a directional annual economic range.
Step 3
Decide where to act first
High-volatility buildings and “watchlist” communities become visible on one page. From there, operators can prioritize pilots, portfolio segmentation, and follow-up analysis by risk band.
